Regional analysis

The image above contains two sets of probability distribution plots analyzing the relationship between cerebral blood flow (CBF), cerebral blood volume (CBV), and coefficient of variation (COV) in patients who have undergone large vessel occlusion (LVO) recanalization.

Figures display the CTP parametric measures on admission within the hypoperfused region for recanalized (Figure left) and non-recanalized (Figure right) patients. The data is visualized through 3D plots (a & c) and 2D projections (b & d). In both types of plots, the first two dimensions represent the values of the parametric measures, while the third dimension in the 3D plots depicts the probability distribution of these value combinations (ranging from 0 to 1). The final infarct is determined as the union of the hypoperfused region identified in the initial CTP scan and the infarcted area observed in the follow-up DWI scan (Step 3 in Figure 7). The color scale in the plots represents the likelihood of final infarction, weighted between 0 and 1, across all subjects. This illustrates the probability of a specific combination of parametric values in the CTP hypoperfused region coinciding with the final infarct area identified on DWI.
